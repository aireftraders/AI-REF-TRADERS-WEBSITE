Conditional Cash Transfer Program

Qualified beneficiaries may receive ₦150,000 through the Federal Government's Conditional Cash Transfer initiative

The Mass Adoption Identity Resource, in collaboration with the Federal Government of Nigeria, is proud to facilitate the identification and registration of qualified beneficiaries for the Conditional Cash Transfer program. This initiative aims to provide financial support to vulnerable households across Nigeria.

Recent updates on the program's progress:

Federal Government Announces 2.3 Million Households Have Received Cash Transfers

The Federal Government has officially confirmed the successful verification and enrollment of 2.3 million Nigerian households in the Conditional Cash Transfer program. This initiative forms part of the government's comprehensive social safety net strategy to mitigate economic challenges faced by citizens.

During a press conference at the National Identity Management Commission (NIMC) headquarters in Abuja, Director General Abisoye Coker-Odusote provided details on the program's implementation:

"Our rigorous validation process has identified 2.3 million qualified households nationwide. Payments to these beneficiaries will commence imminently through our secure digital payment infrastructure," stated Coker-Odusote.

The Director General emphasized the program's commitment to transparency: "Through our partnership with the Mass Adoption Identity Resource, we've implemented robust verification protocols to ensure funds reach only eligible, living beneficiaries. This precision targeting is fundamental to the program's integrity."
